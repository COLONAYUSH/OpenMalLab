# MalAnalyzer - Architecture Review, Round 3 (end-to-end, every standpoint)

> **Method.** Eight independent adversarial reviewers, one per discipline - **security/containment, distributed-systems, AI/ML, licensing/IP, SRE/operability/cost, data/crypto/compliance, product/UX/governance, assurance/testability** - each tasked to *break* the design as it stands after Round 1 (`ARCHITECTURE.md section 8`, four-lens) and Round 2 (`DESIGN-AUDIT.md`, findings A1-A15, residuals R1-R12), and specifically to test **whether the Round-2 fixes introduced new problems.** Every finding below was then independently re-verified against the exact document text before inclusion; reviewer over-claims were dropped. Findings that >=2 lenses reached independently are marked **[convergent]** - the strongest signal they are real.
>
> **On "bulletproof."** It is not an achievable state for a platform that executes live malware and runs LLMs over attacker-authored bytes; VM escape and prompt injection are not fully solvable, and this document does not pretend otherwise. The honest bar - unchanged since Round 1 - is **no unaddressed or silent path: every risk is either closed, or owned as a residual with a named, *gated* validation.** Round 3's blunt result: **the design is not yet at that bar.** It is excellent on the two axes prior rounds drilled (containment topology, copyleft boundary) and materially incomplete on the six they never examined.

---

## 0. Verdict

**Not bulletproof.** Round 3 found **7 critical** and **~15 high** issues that Rounds 1-2 missed, plus a long medium/low tail. They cluster into four meta-patterns, three of which are consequences of Round 2's own fixes:

1. **Round-2 hardened *one* hostile boundary and left its mirror-images open.** The detonation->control result path got a two-process sandbox, strict caps, fuzzing, and an owned residual. Its twin - the **worker->orchestrator `AnalyzeResult` path**, carrying equally hostile-derived data from a producer the design *assumes* is RCE-able (R12), on *every* submission at *every* topology - got none of it. `PHASE1 section 11` even names "the three hostile-input parsers" as extract/ident/pump and **omits the broker.** *(RC-2, convergent: security + assurance.)*
2. **Round-2 fixes *relocated* trust without *neutralizing* it.** A5 moved the store credential to a control-plane broker (but the broker still deserializes attacker-derived protobuf in-process). A3 moved VM-lifecycle state detonation-local (onto **Valkey-alone**, which the project's own `ADR-002` forbids as an authority, and control is now structurally blind to it). A2 moved verdict trust "out-of-band" (to a tap that lives **on the host assumed compromised**). Each move is directionally right and incompletely executed.
3. **The design was engineered to survive a pen-test, not a product / ops / compliance / patent review.** Containment and copyleft-boundary reasoning are above-commercial-grade. But **Day-2 operability, data-lifecycle/erasure, product-wedge, governance mechanism, and patent/FTO** were essentially un-audited until now - and each contains a showstopper for the *exact* markets the spec targets.
4. **The strongest new claims are the least defended.** The sharpest Round-2 corrections (A2 host-forged benign, A3 dead-ended management plane, A1 per-topology dead-end) have **zero CI teeth**, and the external validations that alone could substantiate "most secure" (pen-test, counsel) are **named but not release gates** - only the DR drill is gated. A fix with no test silently regresses. *(RH-1, convergent: assurance.)*

**What this review changes:** a set of unambiguous corrections applied immediately (section 6), a set of design amendments specified for your approval, and a decisions/validations register that must close before any "most secure / compliant / world's-best" claim is made (section 7-section 8).

## 2. Critical findings

### RC-1 - The verdict lattice is mis-ordered; `max` lets BENIGN erase UNKNOWN, making the A2 fix unimplementable  [x] FIXED (section 6)
**[assurance]** - CONFIRMED against text. `PHASE1 section 2.1`: `enum Verdict { UNKNOWN=0; BENIGN=1; SUSPICIOUS=2; MALICIOUS=3; }`; `section 3.1`: `node_verdict = max(own, max(children))`. Numeric `max(UNKNOWN=0, BENIGN=1) = BENIGN`. So an `UNKNOWN` node (e.g. one with an `unanalyzed_region`) with a single benign child rolls up **BENIGN** - benign masking "we don't know," the exact class A2/invariant #8 forbid. A2's own text ("uncorroborated benign detonation => `UNKNOWN`, not `BENIGN`") is **literally impossible** under numeric max on this enum, and the inline comment "UNKNOWN/SUSPICIOUS are the FAIL-CLOSED floors" is false because `BENIGN=1 > UNKNOWN=0`. The section 3.1 worked example only shows MALICIOUS-vs-BENIGN, where `max` is trivially right, and never the UNKNOWN-vs-BENIGN corner where it breaks. **This is the crown-jewel mechanism with a sibling of the very hole it was built to close.** *Fix (applied): re-order to `BENIGN=0 < UNKNOWN=1 < SUSPICIOUS=2 < MALICIOUS=3` so "most-suspicious-wins" `max` gives `join(UNKNOWN,BENIGN)=UNKNOWN`; add an **exhaustive 4x4 property test** of the fail-closed laws. Cheapest, sharpest fix in the review.*

### RC-2 - Worker->broker `AnalyzeResult` is an unguarded hostile-data parse in the trusted plane  [!] AMENDMENT SPECIFIED (section 6 partial)
**[security + assurance, convergent]** - CONFIRMED. The A5 broker runs in the control plane, holds Postgres+vault write authority, and deserializes the worker `AnalyzeResult` protobuf (unbounded `repeated Child children`, `repeated Evidence`, `detail_defanged jsonb`/`rationale jsonb` -> nested/`Struct`-shaped data = exactly the "no `Any`, no nesting" complex deserialization invariant #4 forbids on the detonation path) **in-process**, then persists it as a Temporal activity output re-deserialized on every replay. The producer is *assumed* RCE-able (R12) yet this path - unlike the detonation path - has no caps, no sandbox, no fail-closed-on-malformed, and is **absent from the section 11 fuzz list**. A7 makes it worse: copyleft workers use hand-framed protobuf-over-UDS, so the broker also terminates a length-prefixed framing (classic length-confusion parser) fed by hostile-derived output. *Fix: give boundary (2) the pump's posture - bounded `AnalyzeResult` schema (array/string/total caps <= Temporal limit, field allow-list, no `Any`/`Struct`/nesting), streaming decode in an unprivileged seccomp-strict sub-process, broker + UDS framer added to continuous fuzzing, fail-closed floor on malformed. Applied: doc contract caps + hostile-parser-list + invariant + section 11 case; the sub-process isolation is a build task.*

### RC-3 - The detonation job-*ingress* channel is undefined and self-contradictory across canonical docs  [x] FIXED (section 6)
**[security + distributed, convergent]** - CONFIRMED. `DESIGN-AUDIT A3` says "control enqueues a job to **the pump's outbound spool**; the detonation controller **pulls** it" (spool control-side; detonation initiates). But `diagrams/03` and `10` draw `PUMP -> JOBQ` with the job spool **inside `cluster_det`** (pump reaches in and *writes*), while `diagram 04` says the mover "CANNOT reach orchestrator or vault." These require an **undrawn third pump process** that straddles both planes with a write route *into* detonation - never given the mover/validator least-privilege treatment, and it means a component holds a route into the assume-breach plane. Invariant #9 incoherently lumps outbound "dispatch" into "surfaced to control only by the pump *pulling*." *Fix (applied): pin the A3 model - detonation **pulls** jobs from a **control-side** spool; **all** connection initiation flows detonation->pump; no pump component writes into detonation; diagrams 03/10 and invariant #9 corrected to match.*

### RC-4 - The detonation-local control loop coordinates live VMs on Valkey-alone, violating `ADR-002`, and control is blind to it  [!] AMENDMENT SPECIFIED
**[distributed + security, convergent]** - CONFIRMED. A3 gave the detonation plane its own Valkey lease store + reconciler and denied it Temporal/Postgres - but `ADR-002` states "authoritative run-state lives in Temporal/Postgres, **never Valkey alone**." A Valkey restart wipes all leases -> the reconciler either mass-aborts every in-flight run or rebuilds from libvirt, and in the gap a re-pulled job boots a **second VM for the same run** (double-detonation, which under A9 can let a forged-benign result compete - RH-2). The pull-based "heartbeat" (invariant #9) cannot distinguish *dead* from *unreachable*, so ordinary pull jitter/partition fires a false Temporal heartbeat-timeout -> **re-dispatch of a live detonation** -> double-detonation on heal; and the original `ADR-002` control-side dedup lease is now *impossible* (no Valkey cred into detonation). A compromised controller can also **forge the slot/liveness projection** to flood or stall admission (a new flavor of C-DOS-ROUTER). *Fix: give the detonation plane a **durable single-writer** lifecycle authority (detonation-local Postgres/SQLite-fsync or detonation-local Temporal), bind `run_id`<->domain in libvirt metadata for deterministic idempotent reconcile, move dedup to the spool consumer keyed on `run_id`, decouple heartbeat-timeout from pull latency, and make control-side slot/budget ceilings authoritative (a projection may only *lower* capacity, never raise it).*

### RC-5 - No data-lifecycle / erasure / retention exists; WORM + dedup + DR make lawful deletion impossible  [ ] DECISION + COUNSEL
**[data]** - CONFIRMED (grep: no `delete`/`erasure`/`GDPR`/`retention`/`legal-hold`/`crypto-shred` anywhere; only IOC-decay). WORM from M0. Crypto-shred is defeated because the wrapped DEK sits in a `worm=true` row *and* in every consistency-grouped backup + Shamir-recoverable OpenBao snapshot; and personal data also lives *in cleartext outside the envelope* in `findings`/`evidence`/`detonation_runs.canonical_result`/OpenSearch/Qdrant/graph/`audit_log`. **Adversarial weaponization:** a sample "drops" CSAM or exfiltrated classified bytes -> copied into the WORM vault after pump validation -> you are legally compelled to destroy content in a store engineered to make destruction impossible (S3 Object-Lock COMPLIANCE mode: not even root). This is a legal showstopper for the classified/regulated/EU markets the product explicitly targets. *Fix (decision + counsel): a Data-Lifecycle ADR - move **all** wrapped DEKs out of WORM into a mutable, bounded-retention key catalog so crypto-shred propagates; tombstone + re-index sweep across every derived store on an erasure; a default retention schedule; separate WORM-for-tamper-evidence (hash-chain) from WORM-for-immortality; an adversarial-illegal-content purge runbook; counsel sign-off on whether crypto-erasure satisfies each target regulator.*

### RC-6 - Content-hash dedup is a cross-tenant existence/timing side-channel and contradicts per-tenant keys  [ ] DECISION
**[data]** - CONFIRMED. `samples(sha256 PK ...)` is one global row per hash with global dedup. Tenant B submitting a sample tenant A already holds gets a dedup hit -> instant verdict + `first_seen` = A's timestamp -> **B learns the sample was seen before and when** (in malware IR, that one bit reveals another victim, a broader campaign, or a burned red-team tool; the latency delta leaks it even without a UI signal). It also contradicts `ADR-009` per-tenant KEK + `ADR-016` hard-isolation-default (one ciphertext + one DEK cannot be wrapped under each tenant's separate KEK behind hard isolation), which makes "`tenant_id` from day one => Phase-2 is a migration not a rewrite" **false**. Real strategic tension: cross-tenant corpus intelligence (retrohunt/attribution - a headline feature) vs. cross-tenant confidentiality. *Fix (decision): choose per-trust-domain dedup domains (raw stored+wrapped once per domain; a separately-governed opt-in derivative-only intelligence layer) **or** a shared corpus with a shared corpus-KEK and an honest "sample existence is not confidential in this mode" (mutually exclusive with hard-isolation tenants). Remove `first_seen`/timing observability across trust domains; reconcile the PK/`tenant_id`/KEK story in one ADR.*

### RC-7 - Audit-log tamper-evidence is a SQL comment, not a mechanism  [x] FIXED (section 6)
**[security + data + assurance, convergent - 3 lenses]** - CONFIRMED. `audit_log(... detail jsonb) -- immutable, append-only` is a comment on a plain Postgres table, mutable by the DB owner, a superuser, a `SECURITY DEFINER` function, the newly-empowered broker (RC-2), or anyone restoring a doctored backup. The entire accountability story - verdict-override defensibility (invariant #7), the two-person "accepted exfiltration" cloud record (`ADR-010`), chain-of-custody (asset A6) - rests on it, while the *sample vault* gets WORM+encryption+DR and the *audit log* (the asset most needing tamper-evidence, and the sole detector of insider abuse) gets a comment. For a forensic tool whose output may reach court, a mutable chain of custody is disqualifying. *Fix (applied to spec): hash-chained entries (row n commits `H(n-1)`) + periodic signed checkpoints, written via an INSERT-only non-owner role with `UPDATE`/`DELETE` revoked, mirrored to the WORM store as an independent copy; PII stored as references so erasure (RC-5) removes the referent while the chain stays intact; section 11 tamper-attempt test.*

## 3. High findings (verified, deduped)

- **RH-1 - section 11 doesn't cover the Round-2 fixes; no invariant->test traceability; external validations aren't release gates.** *[assurance]* CONFIRMED. section 11 predates R2; A2/A3/A8/A9/A10/A11/A12 have no tests, so they regress silently. M4 acceptance = "section 11 + DR restore" - **pen-test/red-team and counsel sign-off are named but not gated** (per the reviewer's own rule, "a validation that isn't a release gate doesn't happen"). *Fix (applied to spec): enumerated section 11 additions; a **CI gate failing the build if any invariant #1-#10 or residual R-n lacks a linked test ID**; M4 acceptance amended to require pen-test + counsel sign-off as dated, release-blocking artifacts; a **topology-conformance test** (credential scan + network-reachability probe) that enforces the drawn dead-end instead of asserting it.*
- **RH-2 - Out-of-band verdict trust (A2) is circular at Tier-2.** *[security]* CONFIRMED. The tap/sim-net/attestation are inside the detonation plane on the host A2 assumes is escaped; host-root `setns`/`ptrace`/rewrites the PCAP in host-controlled `RSTORE` before the pump pulls it, and the qcow2 golden image isn't in measured boot -> a compromised host benign-forges *all future* runs routed to it. *Fix (amendment): capture on a **physically separate collector** (HW SPAN/TAP the det host cannot address); evidence must not transit host-controlled storage; bind golden-image + tooling hashes into attestation and have the pump reject attestation-mismatched results; widen R9 from *suppress* to *forge/suppress*; state honestly that Tier-2 verdict integrity assumes an un-escaped host and gate escape-risk samples.*
- **RH-3 - Temporal aggregate limits are adversary-reachable and a terminated workflow bypasses the in-workflow reaper.** *[distributed]* CONFIRMED (A11 only addressed the per-payload blob limit). A 2001-member ZIP exceeds the ~2000 pending-children limit -> workflow **wedges** (not fail-closed); a deep DAG exceeds history size/count -> Temporal **terminates** it -> the in-workflow reaper never fires -> submission left non-finalized (a silent hole). *Fix (amendment): bound *concurrent* pending children/activities well under 2000; `ContinueAsNew` with a bounded carried completion-set; **move the reaper to an external scanner over Postgres** so terminated workflows still finalize-as-incomplete; prove a hard node cap keeps worst-case history < limits; section 11 "2001-member ZIP" + "history-bomb DAG" cases.*
- **RH-4 - Fail-toward-detonation x physical ceiling x no load-shed = verdict-quality collapse at scale, and floods the SOC queue.** *[distributed + product, convergent]* CONFIRMED. *Fix (amendment): risk-tiered, budget-aware warranting (reserve scarce slots for high static-suspicion; cheap Tier-1/defer path for low-signal inconclusives); explicit admission/load-shedding with a distinct non-blocking `deferred_no_capacity` state != `potential_evasion`; a **confidence axis orthogonal to the fail-closed severity floor** so triage stays usable; publish the honest detonation throughput ceiling next to the 1M/day claim.*
- **RH-5 - AI over-claims: "planner never sees raw tokens" is false, "CaMeL" is misbranded, the "deterministic entailment verifier" is reference-checking that launders attacker evidence; RAG contradicts the quarantine; inference substrate isn't isolated.** *[AI]* CONFIRMED (and consistent with my own A13 caveat, but sharper). The planner *must* read attacker-authored free-text values (`config.value`, `note`, `cmdline`) to summarize -> indirect injection survives; a deterministic structured verifier confirms *reference* (nouns) not *predication* (verbs), so a faithful, verifier-green claim can repeat the attacker's "this is a benign Acme tool [cite config:0]" into exported TI; RAG returns raw hostile chunks to the planner (undrawn, contradicts the quarantine) and "artifact scope" is undefined under the recursive DAG -> intra-submission cross-child injection; quarantined and privileged models may share a vLLM/GPU process (an AI analogue of the detonation dead-end that R2 killed elsewhere). *Fix (amendment, mostly wording + two structural): drop "never sees raw tokens"/"CaMeL"/"deterministic entailment"; state the blast-radius cap is the real control; provenance-tag sample-derived evidence as **claims, not facts**, and forbid AI benign-conclusions grounded solely in sample-derived evidence; scope RAG to the single DAG node and route retrieved chunks through the quarantine; **mandate mutually network-isolated inference infrastructure** for quarantined vs privileged models; own a "trojaned/backdoored pinned weights" residual (signing proves provenance, not behavior).*
- **RH-6 - No offline upgrade/rollback/migration design; WORM + append-only fight rollback.** *[SRE]* CONFIRMED (absent). Every upgrade failure at an air-gapped site currently escalates to a full DR event. *Fix (decision + design): expand/contract migrations only within a support window; blue-green control-plane with prior images retained in the same bundle; a "bundle-rejected / migration-failed" runbook that does not require DR; an **offline upgrade + rollback drill** as an acceptance milestone.*
- **RH-7 - Day-2 operability is under-costed; "graph engine/Temporal/OpenBao-HSM" hide multi-service subsystems; the A3 controller is missing from the Phase-1 inventory.** *[SRE]* CONFIRMED. "1 detonation specialist" vs ~20 stateful subsystems (JanusGraph -> Cassandra + index; Temporal -> cluster + DB + visibility store; OpenBao HSM/Shamir -> an unseal ceremony on every restart). *Fix (partly applied): add the A3 detonation-local controller to `PHASE1 section 1` + milestones (applied); publish an honest per-tier system-count + headcount + footprint model; correct "a graph engine (JanusGraph)" to name its backend; consider appliance packaging.*
- **RH-8 - The license CI gate is blind to runtime/`dlopen`/plugin linking, model weights, and content corpora; the inventory is incomplete.** *[licensing]* CONFIRMED. SBOM sees declared deps, not `ctypes.CDLL("libunicorn.so")`, a community plugin that `import qiling` in-process, bundled weights, or bundled goodware. And the blessed matrix omits copyleft engines named as P0/P1: **libemu/scdbg (GPLv2) is a *second* shellcode-emulation path inside the Phase-1 P0 shellcode feature** A7 tried to make copyleft-light; MobSF (GPLv3), Frida, angr->Unicorn(GPLv2) are unlisted. *Fix (applied to spec): ELF/import-graph + runtime-linkage scanning (not just manifests); a plugin license contract (loader refuses to co-load GPL/AGPL into the Apache process); complete the matrix across all phases; "watch: Unicorn transitive"; confirm Phase-1 shellcode uses no copyleft emulator.*
- **RH-9 - A6 (uniform gRPC) contradicts A7 (copyleft != Apache gRPC); diagrams 09<->10 disagree on the transport.** *[licensing]* CONFIRMED. `PHASE1 section 2.1` "every worker implements `service Engine`" + A6 "gRPC over UDS" pulls Apache gRPC; A7 forbids it for copyleft workers. *Fix (applied): split the contract explicitly - permissive workers use gRPC-over-UDS; copyleft workers use hand-framed message-only protobuf or CLI/stdio (a deliberate non-uniform seam); reconcile diagrams 09/10; note this is moot for Phase-1 if emulation is deferred (A7 rec i).*
- **RH-10 - Cross-plane mTLS PKI and embedded-OIDC bootstrap are undefined for the offline deploy - leaving the network route as the sole containment control.** *[security]* CONFIRMED (absent). Bundled static certs/keys -> a leaked image forges plane identity everywhere; flat CA trust -> a compromised detonation identity authenticates to control, so one firewall typo collapses the dead-end. *Fix (design): per-service SPIFFE-style identities with an **authorization** layer (the pump accepts only the detonation identity; control services reject it regardless of CA validity), issued from a per-deployment root generated at first unseal (never bundled); OIDC signing keys per-deployment, out of the shared image.*
- **RH-11 - Bundled model-weight licenses conflict with air-gap (remotely-updatable AUPs), dual-use (anti-malware clauses), and the commercial edition (NC terms).** *[licensing + product]* CONFIRMED. *Fix: a pinned-set filter - redistributable offline + commercial-OK + no remote-AUP dependency + dual-use permitted (likely Apache/MIT-only weights, e.g. Qwen-Apache/OLMo); surface per-model terms at load; model license is a first-class gate input.*
- **RH-12 - No third-party patent / FTO analysis, in a product that reimplements patented commercial-vendor techniques.** *[licensing]* CONFIRMED (Q9 mis-scoped to the internal Apache<->GPL grant). Patents - not copyleft - are the classic open-core killer. *Fix (counsel): FTO opinion on VMI monitoring, HW-breakpoint debugging, CPU-phase tracing, instant-verdict ML before the commercial edition; a real documented clean-room protocol for the CAPE technique.*
- **RH-13 - Governance "no rug-pull like TheHive" is a slogan with no mechanism, and is contradicted by the open-core/CLA posture.** *[product]* CONFIRMED (Q7a-d all open; no foundation, neutral trademark holder, or binding no-clawback clause; open-core intent pushes toward the very CLA/single-entity structure that made TheHive's rug-pull possible). *Fix (founder decision, not counsel): neutral trademark holder / foundation home; DCO (or a narrow reciprocal CLA with an irrevocable OSS grant); a published binding open-core boundary; existing releases stay licensed irrevocably. Until then, drop #9 from the differentiator list.*
- **RH-14 - Phase-1 has no analyst-felt "aha" and serves 1 of 4 promised personas.** *[product]* CONFIRMED. Recut Phase-1 is a thinner CAPE (config/C2 extraction - the reason people run CAPE - is deferred) on *more* hardware (A1's separate node), shipping the tier the product itself calls "trivially detectable" (Tier-2 in-guest), while RE/hunting/detection-eng launch to nothing - so the P0 "four role-based lenses on shared data" is impossible in Phase-1. *Fix (product decision): pull config-extraction+MACO (and/or a detection-eng FP-test loop, and/or offline similarity) into Phase-1 so >=2 personas and a real differentiator ship day one - or reposition Phase-1 honestly as a "compliance-grade air-gapped triage appliance" and re-tag the four lenses as Phase-2.*
- **RH-15 - "Defang everything in exports" contradicts "bidirectional STIX/MISP interchange."** *[product]* CONFIRMED. Defanged indicators aren't machine-matchable; every export becomes a human report, not an interchange artifact. *Fix: split a human-report path (defanged) from a machine-interchange path (real indicators with TLP/marking/`to_ids`, audited channel) + a bulk-export primitive; commit a STIX/MISP round-trip fidelity test.*

## 5. Cross-cutting themes (the through-lines)

1. **Symmetry of hostile-data handling.** The pump got it; the worker->broker path (RC-2), the job-ingress path (RC-3), and content re-hashing (RM-3) did not. *Every producer->trusted-consumer edge* must get the same posture (bounded schema, sandboxed decode, re-hash, fuzz, fail-closed), not just the one that was drawn.
2. **"Never Valkey alone" is violated twice** - the detonation lease store (RC-4) and the recursion-cap counter (RM-6) - the project's own `ADR-002` rule, in the two places correctness depends on it.
3. **Assertion vs. mechanism.** Audit-log immutability (RC-7), out-of-band trust (RH-2), mTLS identity (RH-10), "two-person" (RM-7), and WORM substrate (RM-29) are all *asserted*. Each needs an *engineered* mechanism and a *test*.
4. **Fixes without tests rot.** The invariant->test traceability gate (RH-1) is the single highest-leverage assurance addition: it converts "we forgot to test the fix" from silent to build-blocking, and every Round-2 and Round-3 amendment should land with its test.
5. **Deep on containment/copyleft, shallow on ops/data-lifecycle/product/patent.** The review's center of gravity is the six standpoints prior rounds never covered; that's where the showstoppers for the *target market* live.

## 10. Honest closing verdict

**MalAnalyzer is not bulletproof - and after three rounds, the right conclusion is that "bulletproof" is the wrong target.** The correct target is the one the docs already set: *no unaddressed or silent path, with owned residuals and gated validations.* Measured against that, the design is **strong and honest on containment topology and the copyleft boundary** - genuinely better than most commercial tools - and **materially incomplete on hostile-boundary symmetry, distributed-coordination durability, data lifecycle/compliance, assurance/testing, operability, product wedge, governance, and patent.**

The most important truth this round surfaces is structural, not any single bug: **Round 2's fixes were the right *moves* but were executed against one boundary and one threat, and the same rigor was not propagated to the mirror boundaries or re-derived after the plane surgery.** Fixing RC-1 through RC-7 and standing up the RH-1 traceability gate would restore the "no silent path" bar for the *engineered* system; closing the section 7 decisions (data-lifecycle, tenancy, product, governance) and section 8 gated validations is what would let the project honestly make its market claims. None of it requires abandoning the architecture - the three-plane model, the dead-end, the fail-closed verdict fabric, and the quarantine all survive Round 3 intact. What they need is symmetry, durability, mechanism-where-there-is-assertion, tests-where-there-is-prose, and the five non-security standpoints treated as first-class. Until then: **airtight on the boundaries that were drawn; porous on the ones that weren't - and silent on the markets it means to serve.**
